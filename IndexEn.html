Gymnastics

Much of my childhood was shaped by a single decision when I was five years old. This was the young age where I made the decision to start the only sport in which I could give 100% of my energy. Gymnastics. This first step, coupled with less than 5 years later the important question of whether or not I wanted to pursue the dream of every young athlete, the Olympic Games, were the ingredients of a youth with constant perseverance and uncompromising discipline.
At the age of ten, with youthful energy, with a huge dream, and now also with a elite sport document in my pocket, I started the adventure of spending 30 hours a week in a gym to achieve that dream.
What I did not take into account at that age is how difficult it was going to make some aspects of my life.
With a clear planning and the willingness of different authorities I balanced elite sport gymnastics with my studies in Sciences at the Voskenslaan in Ghent.
The real difficulties in my sports career played out after secondary school. In achieving my intermediate goal towards those Olympics, participating a final at the World Championships in 2015, I tore my Achilles tendon. This is where my perseverance and my "keep on going" mentality was first tested.
In the years that followed, I underwent surgeries on both shoulders, tore ligaments in both feet and had several setbacks in my shoulders. Despite these hard setbacks, my story of being in the Olympics still hasn't passed.

Education

My many setbacks in my sports career have ensured that I always had a plan B at my disposal. After my first injury, I started on my academic career. After misfiring twice, in 2016 I finally had the direction where my passion was divided between my gymnastics and my education.
Applied computer science at HoGent has sucked me completely into the world of technology.
